BMW Group Internship: E INK

I had a 20 Week Internship at BMW in the Driving Simulation Center in Munich, working on a daily basis with the Unreal Engine 5, C++ on a complex project in a Computergraphics Topic, in regards to the E INK Topic from ground up.

I made E-Ink visually experiencable, inside the Unreal Engine across all vehicles. New Vehicles which are added, are supported instantly.

During my development process of an powerfull, custom generic algorithm, which supports any ,dynamically extracted, surface out of the box, i gained much more experience in working with MeshData Buffers, Projection and Matrixcalculations: solving Computergraphics related problems all in C++.

On top of working with alot of MeshData, i had to develop a gui-framework in C++ for UCanvas from scratch, because the Unreal Engine has a weak UCanvas interface.

Working on this agile research / exploration project, as the only Software-Engineer, Programmer and Designer at the same time, was challenging but a really great opportunity to gain more Experience!

Pathfinding: A* Algorithm

I developed a pathfinding solution for the Unreal Engine, based on the A* Algorithm for 3D environments, utilizing a 2D grid map to store nodes. These nodes are either pre-collected from meshes at the start of the game or dynamically added asynchronously during gameplay. This ensures that a route, such as one guiding a bot to the player, is always successfully determined.
